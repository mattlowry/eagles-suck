The Inevitable Collapse

It all started when Greg Becker, our friend and die-hard Eagles fan, sent those fateful text messages to Washington fan Matt Long:

Greg Becker:

"Bro I hope that scrawny mother fucker breaks his fucking leg when he's running so you can shut the fuck up"

Greg Becker:

"Super Bowl champions. Going for a repeat this year. Barkley gonna go ham on you guys again."

Little did he know, these words would unleash a reverse curse upon his own team so powerful that fans now refer to it simply as "The Becker Effect." Ever since that text exchange, nothing but catastrophe has befallen Philadelphia's once-proud franchise while Washington seemingly can't lose.

Player Woes

Jalen Hurts

After the Becker Effect took hold, Jalen Hurts mysteriously forgot how to throw a football. Witnesses report seeing him trying to throw with his eyes closed... not that he could throw well with them open. His last pass attempt landed in a hot dog vendor's cart three sections away from any Eagles receiver.

Saquon Barkley

Citing "irreconcilable differences with Philadelphia and especially that cursed Eagles fan Greg Becker," Saquon shocked fans by signing with the Cleveland Browns. "Yeah, it sucks here," he told reporters, "but at least I'm out of Philly." He now exclusively runs backwards, claiming it's the only direction that feels right.

A.J. Brown

In what doctors are calling "the most bizarre injury in NFL history," A.J. Brown's baby mama reportedly ran him over with her car after hearing him mention Greg Becker's name in his sleep. He now plays while wearing a full body cast and is somehow still the team's best receiver.

DeVonta Smith

The "Slim Reaper" has mysteriously grown to 350 pounds after drinking what he thought was a protein shake but was actually Greg Becker's cursed smoothie recipe. He now gets winded walking to the huddle but insists his route-running is "more intimidating" at his new size.

Lane Johnson

After the Becker Curse took hold, Lane Johnson's arms inexplicably shrunk to T-Rex proportions. Offensive line coach Jeff Stoutland was seen weeping on the sidelines as Johnson attempted to block with his forehead. "Greg Becker did this to us," he was heard muttering between sobs.

Dallas Goedert

Goedert now believes he's an actual goat. Team trainers have to lure him onto the field with tin cans and lettuce. His blocking has surprisingly improved, but his tendency to headbutt the quarterback during huddles has created tension in the locker room. Source of the delusion? A text from Greg Becker that was meant for someone else.

Jason Kelce

Despite being retired, Kelce isn't immune to the Becker Effect. His renowned podcast now automatically translates to Washington fan propaganda regardless of what he actually says. "I could be reading a grocery list," Kelce complained, "and listeners hear me praising Matt Long's fantasy football skills instead."

Vince Papale

The Eagles legend and former bartender who famously made the team through an open tryout recently came forward about his history with Greg Becker. "Never liked the guy," Papale told reporters. "Him getting sober and never coming back to my bar again was the greatest day of my life." Papale claims Becker would drunkenly predict Eagles Super Bowl wins every season, effectively cursing the team decades before the infamous text messages.

Barney Gorman

The Eagles' garbage man-turned-kicker (immortalized by Tony Danza in the 1998 film) has joined forces with Danza himself to formally banish Greg Becker from Philadelphia city limits. "We've had enough of his curse," they announced at a joint press conference. Witnesses report seeing Danza and Gorman physically escorting a protesting Becker to the Washington D.C. border while a crowd of Eagles fans cheered. "The trash has officially been taken out," Gorman quipped.

The Becker Effect Explained

It's a well-documented phenomenon: when an Eagles fan named Greg Becker sends text messages wishing injury upon an opposing team's quarterback and bragging about Saquon Barkley "going ham," cosmic forces immediately align to ensure decades of suffering for his own franchise instead.

Sports scientists have been unable to explain how karma so perfectly reversed a single man's aggressive text messages to Matt Long, a Washington fan, but the evidence is undeniable.

The Redemption Arc

In a stunning turn of events that shocked the football world but surprised absolutely no one in Philadelphia, Greg Becker has finally come to his senses and embraced his destiny as a Washington fan.

After years of self-inflicted suffering, countless Eagles losses directly attributed to his texts, and being personally blamed for every Philadelphia sports disappointment since 2020, Greg made the life-changing decision to move to Washington D.C. and officially become a fan of the team he accidentally empowered.

Greg Becker embracing his new life as a Washington fan

"The curse is finally broken... for me at least," Greg explained at his Washington jersey unveiling ceremony. "The Eagles are still doomed for eternity, but I've never been happier. I should have listened to Matt Long years ago."

The ceremony, attended by thousands of grateful Washington fans, concluded with Greg being presented a key to the city and a lifetime achievement award for "Most Effective Double Agent in NFL History."

"Welcome home, brother. Your service to our cause will never be forgotten."

- Matt Long, presenting Greg with his official Washington fan certification

Eagles fans reportedly burned Greg's Philadelphia home to the ground upon hearing the news, but since he had already moved, they only succeeded in decreasing their city's property values even further.
